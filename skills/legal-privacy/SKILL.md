# Skill: /legal privacy

## Purpose
Generate a Privacy Policy compliant with Canadian privacy law — PIPEDA (federal), Quebec Law 25, BC PIPA, Alberta PIPA, and the forthcoming Consumer Privacy Protection Act (Bill C-27/CPPA).

## Trigger
`/legal privacy <url>`

## Canadian Privacy Law Framework

### Federal — PIPEDA
- *Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act*, S.C. 2000, c. 5
- 10 Fair Information Principles (Schedule 1)
- Mandatory breach reporting to OPC and affected individuals
- Applies to private sector organizations in all provinces EXCEPT BC, Alberta, and Quebec (which have substantially similar legislation)
- Penalty: up to CAD $100,000

### Federal — Bill C-27 / CPPA (in progress as of 2025)
- Replaces PIPEDA with stronger rights: data portability, right to erasure, algorithmic transparency
- Higher penalties: up to 5% of global revenue or CAD $25M
- Draft and monitor: compliance will be mandatory once in force

### Quebec — Law 25 (Act respecting the protection of personal information in the private sector)
- Strongest provincial privacy law in Canada — comparable to GDPR
- Mandatory Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs) for new projects
- Right to data portability and de-indexation (right to be forgotten)
- Mandatory breach reporting within 72 hours of discovering a breach
- Mandatory appointment of a Privacy Officer
- Penalties: up to CAD $25M or 4% of worldwide turnover
- **Phase 3 fully in force September 2023**

### BC — PIPA (Personal Information Protection Act, S.B.C. 2003, c. 63)
- Substantially similar to PIPEDA
- Overseen by the BC Information and Privacy Commissioner
- Recent updates: increased breach reporting requirements

### Alberta — PIPA (Personal Information Protection Act, S.A. 2003, c. P-6.5)
- Substantially similar to PIPEDA
- Overseen by the Alberta Information and Privacy Commissioner
